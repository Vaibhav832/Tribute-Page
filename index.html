Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam


Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.

About the Legend

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).

In 1969, he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India. Rejoining DRDO in 1982, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname "Missile Man."

Kalam was a visionary leader and a source of inspiration for millions of people around the world. He was known for his humility, dedication, and passion for science and technology. His contributions to India's space and defense programs have left a lasting impact on the country's progress.

Apart from his scientific achievements, Kalam was also an acclaimed author, with several books to his name. He was a great advocate for education, particularly in the fields of science and technology, and emphasized the importance of nurturing young minds and encouraging innovation.

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's legacy continues to inspire future generations to dream big and work towards a better tomorrow. He will always be remembered as a true visionary and a remarkable personality who dedicated his life to the service of the nation.
